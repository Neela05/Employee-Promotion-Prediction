# Employee-Promotion-Prediction# 

##  Project Overview

Employee promotion is a critical process in every organization. Identifying employees who are eligible for promotion based on performance, training, and other factors can be time-consuming and challenging.

This project leverages **Machine Learning** to predict whether an employee is likely to be promoted based on historical employee data. The model helps HR departments make data-driven promotion decisions and improve workforce management.

---

## Objective

The objective of this project is to:

- Predict employee promotion eligibility.
- Assist HR teams in decision-making.
- Reduce manual effort in promotion evaluation.
- Improve organizational productivity through data-driven insights.

## Dataset Features

The dataset contains employee-related information such as:

- Department
- Education
- Gender
- Recruitment Channel
- Number of Trainings
- Previous Year Rating
- KPI Achievement
- Awards Won
- Age
- Length of Service
- Promotion Status (Target Variable)

---

##  Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Several visualizations were performed to understand employee promotion patterns.

### Key Findings

###  KPI Achievement
Employees achieving more than 80% KPI scores had a higher probability of promotion.

###  Department Analysis
- Sales & Marketing had the highest number of employees.
- Procurement and Technology departments showed higher promotion rates.

###  Education
Employees with Bachelor's degrees were more likely to receive promotions.

###  Performance Ratings
Employees with a rating of 5 had the highest promotion probability.

###  Age Group
Employees between 25 and 40 years old showed greater chances of promotion.

###  Awards
Employees receiving awards were promoted more frequently than those without awards.

##  Results

The Decision Tree model successfully predicted employee promotion eligibility based on employee performance and organizational factors.

### Important Insights

✅ KPI achievement significantly influences promotion decisions.

✅ Employees with higher performance ratings are more likely to be promoted.

✅ Awards and training participation positively impact promotion probability.

✅ Department and recruitment channel have relatively less influence on promotion outcomes.
